🔴 Chronic Distress
Scenario description:
Often serves as the trigger mechanism for other scenarios. Associated with intoxication and detox pathways, microbiome health, chronic psycho-emotional stress and lifestyle factors.
Key programme focus:
Detox support + microbiome restoration + lifestyle work (sleep/rest) + stress-reduction techniques.
Indicators considered: Nervous system (1.1–1.8), GI tract (2–3), intoxication (4), immunity (10), ferritin, vitamin D, TSH, homocysteine, testosterone, total protein/albumin
🟠 Chronic Inflammation
Scenario description:
Imbalance and dysfunction of the immune system. Neuroinflammation, autoimmune thyroiditis, persistent immune dysregulation, chronic allergic reactions, pronounced GI symptoms, chronic tissue and organ inflammation.
Key programme focus:
Often triggered by the distress scenario; leads to hormonal imbalances, reduced immune function and tissue inflammation.
Indicators considered: GI (3), respiratory (11), cardiovascular (5), allergies (4.6), thyroid (9.1), CRP, homocysteine, vitamin D, triglycerides, LDL cholesterol
🟣 Mitochondrial Dysfunction
Scenario description:
All remaining dysfunctions — the body is depleted, deficiencies have accumulated and cannot be resolved by simple supplementation. The dysfunction is chronic at the mitochondrial level, typically affecting the most energy-demanding organs: thyroid, brain, immune system, pancreas, heart, kidneys, liver.
Key programme focus:
Can be triggered by any scenario, most commonly distress. Manifests as acute impairment of cellular energy production; worsened by nutritional deficiencies and poor GI function.
Indicators considered: Fatigue (1.1, 4.4), sleep disturbance, cognitive function (1.6), musculoskeletal (8), thyroid (9.1–9.2), respiratory (11), total protein, haemoglobin, RBC, ferritin, TSH, T3, T4, vitamin D, urea/creatinine
🔵 Insulin Resistance
Scenario description:
Often triggered by distress or inflammation. Disruption of carbohydrate metabolism as the primary breakdown mechanism, impaired glucose adaptation, visceral fat accumulation, metabolic disorders, weight problems, pre-diabetes and type 2 diabetes — frequently co-existing with impaired bile flow.
Key programme focus:
Can be triggered by any scenario, often by distress or inflammation. Diet plays a central role, as does excess weight. Directly linked to bile flow and GI detox function.
Indicators considered: Endocrine (9.2), cardiovascular (5.3, 5.6, 5.10), nutrition (12), visceral fat (M6.5), glucose, HbA1c, insulin, C-peptide, triglycerides, HDL, LDL, VLDL, cholesterol, ALT, ALP, urea/creatinine, testosterone
